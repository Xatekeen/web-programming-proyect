Life at Sejong

Student Services, Campus Support & Daily Life

Everything you need to thrive at Sejong — from housing and healthcare to career support, clubs, and IT services for international students.

Students

Student Council

Representative student bodies coordinating campus activities, advocacy, and academic governance for Sejong's student community.

Global

International Student Organizations

Support networks and cultural groups helping global students adapt to life and study in Seoul — with immigration guidance and community events.

Campus

Facilities

Libraries, sports halls, lecture theatres, student lounges, cafeterias, and service offices distributed across the Gwangjin-gu campus.

Career

Career & Employment

Job fairs, internship placements, career counselling, and on-campus employment opportunities — including the annual International Student Futures fair.

Living

Dormitory

On-campus housing for domestic and international students with options for single, double, and shared rooms. Applications open each semester.

Health

Insurance & Hospitals

Information on the national health insurance scheme for international students, campus health centre services, and nearby partner hospitals.

Clubs

Club Activities

Over 100 registered student clubs covering sports, arts, culture, technology, volunteering, and academic interest groups open to all students.

Legal

Immigration & Visa

Guidance on visa applications, extensions, and status changes for international students — including group processing support through the International Office.

Service

IT Service

Electronic attendance system, student ID card issuance, academic certificates, transcript requests, and full technology support for enrolled students.

Student Guidebook

Your First Step at Sejong

The Guidebook for Students is available in English and Korean, covering registration, housing, insurance, and campus orientation for new international students.

Orientation & Registration

All new international students attend a mandatory orientation session covering academic registration, ID card collection, insurance enrolment, and campus familiarisation.

Parking & Transport

Campus parking is available for registered vehicles. The campus is also accessible via Seoul Metro (Gunja Station, Lines 5 & 7) and multiple bus routes.

Document & Transcript Services

Enrolled students can request official certificates, transcripts, and enrolment confirmations online or in person through the Academic Affairs office.
